RDR2 "Free DLC" for July: What Rockstar Actually Announced

On July 1, 2026, Rockstar opened a Red Dead Online event called Bounty Hunter Bonuses. Several outlets reported it as free DLC for Red Dead Redemption 2. The event adds no missions and no modes. Here is its exact contents, how it was reported, and what Rockstar has said about Red Dead Online since 2022.

July 16, 2026 By Joseph Lambert 6 min read

Rockstar's Newswire post announcing the event is titled "Band Together to Take Down Criminals and Earn Bounty Hunter Bonuses". The event runs from July 1 to August 3, 2026. Rockstar describes it in one sentence: "Bring dangerous criminals across the frontier to heel and earn 3X RDO$, XP, and Role XP dragging in Regular Bounties, and 3X RDO$, XP, and Gold hauling in Legendary and Infamous Bounties."

What the July 2026 event contains

The full contents, as listed by Rockstar and relayed by RockstarINTEL on June 30, 2026:

  • Regular Bounties pay 3X RDO$, XP and Role XP. Legendary and Infamous Bounties pay 3X RDO$, XP and Gold.
  • Day of Reckoning and Manhunt Free Roam Events pay 3X RDO$ and XP.
  • Posse XP is boosted by 100%, and the Persistent Posse setup fee is waived.
  • The Bounty Hunter Wagon is free. Bounty Hunter Licenses are 5 gold bars off. Wagon tints, outfits and weapons are 50% off.
  • Monthly rewards include the Seek Trouble emote, the Orange Raccoon Skin Hat and a free weapon voucher.
  • Weekly rotating rewards include the Hawkmoth Bolas, the Eberhart Coat, the West Hill Haven Treasure Map and the Raccoon Hat.
  • Logging in during July grants the Rebellion Poncho. Players with the Bounty Hunter role receive the Spirit of 1776 Bandana.

Every activity in that list predates the event. The Bounty Hunter role launched on September 10, 2019 with the Frontier Pursuits update, alongside the Trader and Collector roles. The event changes payout multipliers and prices on content that already shipped. It adds no mission, no mode, no region and no role.

How it was reported

ScreenRant published "Red Dead Redemption 2 Free DLC For July Officially Announced" on July 1, 2026. On July 10, the same outlet published "Red Dead Redemption 2 Is Officially Getting New Content In 2026". That second article names no new content. Its forward-looking claim is that "with Rockstar expected to launch Grand Theft Auto VI later this year, its attention may soon return to the Red Dead franchise".

ScreenRant has applied the DLC framing to every monthly Red Dead Online event of 2026, in January, February, March, April, May and June. CBR ran "Red Dead Redemption 2 DLC Officially 100% Free for March 2026" in March.

Rockstar's own post uses the word "Bonuses". It does not use the words "DLC" or "new content". GameRant covered the same event on June 30, 2026 under the headline "New Red Dead Online Bonuses Are Live Now", and noted that Rockstar wound down the game's support in 2022.

The Rebellion Poncho is a returning item

The Rebellion Poncho is granted for logging in during July 2026. It was also granted for logging in during July 2025, and during July 2024. Worthplaying reported on July 2, 2024: "Log in between July 2 – 8 to receive the Rebellion Poncho for free."

The item itself is older. It originated in Outlaw Pass No. 2 at rank 96, and is sold by Madame Nazar for $385. It is an Independence Day themed cosmetic that Rockstar re-issues each July.

What Rockstar said in July 2022

On July 7, 2022, Rockstar published a Newswire post titled "Grand Theft Auto and Red Dead Online Community Update". On the future of Red Dead Online, it said: "we plan to build upon existing modes and add new Telegram Missions this year, rather than delivering major themed content updates like in previous years".

On its reasons, it said: "Over the past few years, we have been steadily moving more development resources towards the next entry in the Grand Theft Auto series". Rockstar wrote "the next entry in the Grand Theft Auto series". It did not write GTA 6.

Kotaku titled its July 7, 2022 article "Rockstar: No Major Updates Coming For Red Dead Online". VGC titled its July 8 article "Rockstar says major Red Dead Online updates will stop as it focuses on GTA 6". Rockstar's sentence ruled out one category, "major themed content updates", and scoped it to that year. This site covered the announcement in July 2022.

What Red Dead Online has received since

Rockstar said it would add Telegram Missions rather than themed updates, and that is what happened. The record since July 2022:

  • September 6, 2022. Three Hardcore Telegram Missions, known as Skelding's Contract, plus new variations of the Rich Pickings and Outrider missions.
  • October 18, 2022. One Halloween Hardcore Telegram Mission, False Hopes & Prophecy.
  • August 2023. Three Telegram Missions: Hostage to Fortune, The Bell Tolls and Trial & Tribulation.
  • October 2024. Three Bodyguard Telegram Missions, for Halloween.
  • July 1, 2025. Strange Tales of the West Vol. 1, four Telegram Missions built around Theodore Levin, a character from RDR2's story mode. Logged as title update 1.32.

No new role, mode or region has shipped since July 2022. The most recent new content is the July 2025 mission set. No new mission has been identified in 2026. Red Dead Redemption 2's story mode has received no content since its release on October 26, 2018.

What it says about RDR3

Rockstar has not announced Red Dead Redemption 3. The July 2026 event concerns Red Dead Online and refers to no future Red Dead game. ScreenRant's July 10 article connects the two through an expectation rather than an announcement, stating that Rockstar's attention "may soon return to the Red Dead franchise" once Grand Theft Auto VI ships.

Take-Two's figures put Red Dead Redemption 2 past 70 million units as of February 2025. Rockstar has made no public statement linking those sales to a third game.
