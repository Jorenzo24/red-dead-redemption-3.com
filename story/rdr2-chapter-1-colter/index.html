Chapter 1: Colter

Red Dead Redemption 2 Chapter 1 of 6 Colter, Ambarino 1899

By Joseph Lambert · Updated June 24, 2026

Chapter 1 of Red Dead Redemption 2 opens in the worst possible moment for the Van der Linde gang. A robbery in the town of Blackwater has just gone catastrophically wrong, and the survivors have fled north into the frozen Grizzly Mountains of Ambarino to escape the law. It is a bleak, white-knuckle prologue that introduces the gang, its desperation, and the man we play as: Arthur Morgan.

Spoilers ahead for the opening of Red Dead Redemption 2.

Setting the scene

The gang has lost people and money in Blackwater, and a late spring blizzard traps them, half-frozen and starving, in the abandoned mining town of Colter. Two wounded members, Davey Callander and Jenny Kirk, die from injuries sustained in the escape and are buried in the snow. Morale is at rock bottom, and tempers are short.

What happens

From the freezing shacks of Colter, Dutch van der Linde tries to hold the gang together while it waits out the storm. Tensions flare immediately: the newcomer Micah Bell and Bill Williamson nearly come to blows before Dutch redirects everyone's anger toward a familiar enemy, the rival outlaw Colm O'Driscoll.

Venturing out into the blizzard, Arthur helps rescue John Marston, who has been left gravely wounded after a wolf attack while scouting. The gang then discovers a nearby O'Driscoll camp and learns the rival gang has been planning to rob a train. In the raid that follows, Arthur captures a young, terrified O'Driscoll named Kieran Duffy and drags him back to camp for questioning.

Pushing back out into the cold, the gang also finds a homesteader's wife hiding at a ranch the O'Driscolls have just raided: Sadie Adler, whose husband has been murdered. They take her in. Finally, turning the O'Driscolls' own scheme against them, the gang robs a train belonging to the powerful industrialist Leviticus Cornwall, making an enemy who will haunt them for the rest of the story, but also earning the money they need to move.

Key characters introduced

Colter is essentially the gang's roll call. It establishes Arthur Morgan as the protagonist and Dutch's most trusted enforcer, reintroduces John Marston, and plants the seeds of trouble with Micah Bell. It also marks the arrival of Sadie Adler, one of the game's most beloved figures, and Kieran Duffy, the captured O'Driscoll who will slowly earn the gang's trust.

Why it matters

Colter is short, but it does the heavy lifting for the whole game. It strips the gang down to nothing, snow, hunger, grief, so that everything they rebuild afterward feels earned, and everything they lose later hurts. It establishes the O'Driscoll feud, the Cornwall vendetta, and the fragile bonds that the rest of Red Dead Redemption 2 will test to destruction. With the weather finally breaking, the gang rides down out of the mountains to a new camp at Horseshoe Overlook, and the real story begins.
